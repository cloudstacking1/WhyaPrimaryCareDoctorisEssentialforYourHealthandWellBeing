What is Primary Care Doctor?

A Primary Care Doctor is a healthcare professional who provides comprehensive, ongoing care, acting as the first point of contact for health concerns. They are responsible for diagnosing and treating common illnesses, managing chronic conditions, and offering preventive care to maintain overall health. Primary care doctors specialize in areas like family medicine, internal medicine, pediatrics, or geriatrics, depending on the patients age group and health needs. They play a crucial role in coordinating care, offering guidance on healthy living, and referring patients to specialists when needed. Regular visits to a primary care doctor ensure continuity of care, early detection of potential health issues, and better management of long-term conditions.
